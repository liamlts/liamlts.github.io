title: "Strain Engineering of Quantum Materials"

Epitaxial strain provides a powerful knob for tuning the magnetic and electronic ground states of quantum material thin films. In α-MnTe, I have shown that substrate-induced strain modifies the Mn local environment, shifts dd excitations measured by RIXS, and controls the magnitude of the anomalous Hall effect. EXAFS analysis at the Mn K-edge quantifies these structural distortions at the sub-angstrom level, revealing bond-length changes that correlate with the strain-dependent magnetic properties. This approach extends to other systems including magnetocaloric AlFe₂B₂ and frustrated magnet thin films.
