Van der Waals magnets offer a platform for exploring magnetism in the two-dimensional limit, with applications in spintronics and quantum information. I investigate the magnetic anisotropy, exchange interactions, and electronic structure of layered magnetic compounds — including CuCrP₂S₆ and AgCrP₂S₆ — using XMCD and RIXS at the Cr L-edge. These measurements reveal how interlayer coupling, ligand environment, and spin-orbit effects shape magnetic order in reduced dimensions.
