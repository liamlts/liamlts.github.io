abstract: "α-MnTe is a prototypical altermagnet exhibiting a strong anomalous Hall effect (AHE), despite having a nearly vanishing magnetization. By studying the scaling of the AHE with conductivity for films and previously reported measurements in the literature, we find that sample-to-sample variations are well explained by a scaling law consistent with a hopping origin. A comparison with other magnetic semiconductors reveals the colossal amplitude of the AHE of α-MnTe compared to its measured spontaneous magnetization. Our findings address the important fundamental question of the origin of the AHE of α-MnTe and further demonstrate the potential of altermagnets as promising spintronic materials."
